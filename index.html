1990s-2000s


This was the period when India experienced a consumer boom, which continues to this day. During this time, international brands and multinational corporations such as Coca-Cola, Pepsi, McDonald's, and various apparel and product brands entered the Indian market. Their marketing efforts and products had a significant impact on consumer preferences, which kept evolving and continues to.

But how did foreign brands appeal to such a diverse audience as India's?

Glocalization, the combination of globalization and localization, is a concept that describes the adaptation of global products and services to suit local tastes, culture, and preferences.

Glocalization played a significant role in the rise of consumerism in India by blending global and local elements in products, services, and marketing strategies. This approach catered to the diverse and culturally rich Indian market.
